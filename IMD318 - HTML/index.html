A brief history

During my primary school years, I attended three different schools because my father's job required him to transfer to new places. It was a bit challenging at first, but it also allowed me to adapt to new environments and meet many wonderful people. Each school brought unique experiences and opportunities, and I'm grateful for the variety it added to my childhood.

Of course, moving around meant there were both good and bad memories. On the positive side, I made a lot of friends and got to explore different cultures and traditions in each place. On the flip side, it was tough saying goodbye to friends and adjusting to new routines every time we moved.

For secondary school, I was fortunate to stay in one place, which gave me a sense of stability. It allowed me to build stronger, long-lasting friendships and focus more on my studies and personal growth. Like anyone, I had my ups and downs, but overall, my secondary school journey was filled with valuable lessons, unforgettable moments, and opportunities to discover my interests and passions.

Currently, I’m pursuing a diploma, and it’s been an eye-opening experience so far. Compared to my earlier school years, this stage of education feels more focused and practical, as I get to dive deeper into subjects that align with my future goals. It’s not always easy, but I find the challenges to be worth it because they push me to grow and improve. Studying for my diploma has also allowed me to meet people from different backgrounds, which has been inspiring. I’ve learned a lot, not just academically but also about teamwork, time management, and balancing responsibilities.

Overall, those experiences shaped me into a more flexible and open-minded person.
